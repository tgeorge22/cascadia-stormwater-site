§ 02 / Method

Why contractors keep calling back.

A handful of working principles shape every inspection, every plan markup, and every corrective-action call from the site.

01 Field-first

Built for real conditions, not paper compliance.

Plans that survive contact with a wet site, a fresh subgrade, and a crew that needs to be moving. If a BMP can't be installed correctly in the mud, it doesn't get specified.

02 Fast response

Corrective actions within the same shift.

When a silt fence fails or a wattle gets flattened, the fix happens before the next forecast hits — not at the next scheduled inspection.

03 Clean record

Documentation agencies can't pick apart.

Inspection logs, rainfall tracking, corrective actions, and photo evidence organized the way DEQ and DOE actually review them.

04 Shared language

Translation between crew and agency.

Field superintendents and permit reviewers do not speak the same dialect. We handle the translation so neither side wastes time.

05 Budget-aware

Mitigation scaled to site risk.

A 4-acre flat commercial pad does not need the same BMP suite as a 22% cut slope above a salmon-bearing creek. We match controls to actual discharge risk.

06 Close-out discipline

NOT filed before the equipment leaves.

Final stabilization walks, termination packages, and retention of records handled so projects release liability instead of trailing it.
